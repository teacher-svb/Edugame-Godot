# EduGame: Mabel the Engineer - Chapter Breakdown

## Act I: Welcome to Beaverford (Setup)
*   **Chapter 1: Morning in Beaverford.** Mabel wakes up and explores the village. The player sees the "Backlog of Broken Things" and hears villagers' dependence on the busy Wizard.
*   **Chapter 2: The Ambition.** Mabel decides to help her neighbors, but is rejected because she isn't a "real" magician. She decides to apply to the Wizard School.
*   **Chapter 3: The Wizard School Trials.** Mabel visits different elemental wizards (Fire, Water, Earth). She fails every "Spark Test" while others succeed.
*   **Chapter 4: The Heavy Heart.** The realization that she has zero magical potential. She is sent home, walking through the forest near the guarded Mana Spring.

## Act II: Spellcasting Without a Wand (Hook)
*   **Chapter 1: The Desperate Help.** Back in Beaverford, Mabel finds a villager in a crisis that magic haven't solved yet. She uses a simple math/physical fix.
*   **Chapter 2: The Rumor Mill.** The villager is amazed and spreads the word: "Mabel can cast spells without a wand!" The village celebrates their new "prodigy."
*   **Chapter 3: The Busy Week.** Mabel goes around town solving the "Backlog" using engineering tools, while the villagers cheer for her "Invisible Magic."
*   **Chapter 4: The Exposure.** A traveling Wizard from the school arrives. He tests Mabel and publicly declares her a "fraud" with no mana. Mabel retreats to her home in shame.
*   **Chapter 5: The Mysterious Letter.** A gear-sealed letter is left for her: an invitation from the Secret Guild of Engineers.

## Act III: The Guild of Engineers (Development)
*   **Chapter 1: The Hidden Workshop.** Mabel finds the secret entrance to the Guild. She meets her new mentors and sees technology for the first time.
*   **Chapter 2: The Night Shift.** Mabel begins fixing the town at night. The village is dark and quiet while everyone sleeps. She works alone with her new tools.
*   **Chapter 3: The Ghost of Beaverford.** The villagers are confused—the Wizard is gone, yet things are being fixed. They start attributing the work to the "Silent Wizard."
*   **Chapter 4: The Flickering World.** The enchanted streetlights begin to sputter. Mabel notices the world's magic is failing. She is tasked to investigate.

## Act IV: The Secret of the Wizards (Turn)
*   **Chapter 1: The Great Blackout.** The magic fails completely. The world is pitch black. Mabel builds her first "Mechanical Lantern" (The Engineering Light).
*   **Chapter 2: The Unguarded Forest.** With her lantern, Mabel navigates the dark woods. Since the wizards have no light, the Mana Spring's entrance is unguarded.
*   **Chapter 3: The Mana Mine.** Mabel finds the clearing where magic is being "mined" from the earth.
*   **Chapter 4: The Master of Gears Reveal.** Mabel finds the Village Wizard leading the Guild! He is using machines to "pump" the last of the mana.
*   **Chapter 5: The Realization.** The Wizard asks her to help find a new mana vein. Mabel refuses — and sees the full truth: he controlled both magic and the Guild, ensuring the village always depended on him. Unable to accept a world without that control, he leaves alone. By morning, his house is empty.

## Act V: Everyone Can Do Magic (Resolution)
*   **Chapter 1: The Great Conversion.** Mabel returns and replaces the "Mana-Extractor" with a mechanical "Water-Pump" and "Wind-Turbine."
*   **Chapter 2: The Lesson.** Mabel teaches the villagers how to use the machines. The "mystery" of fixing things is gone—it's just logic.
*   **Chapter 3: The Arcanum Shift.** The Wizard School is rebranded. The "Grimoires" are replaced by "Blueprints."
*   **Chapter 4: Professor Mabel.** A final scene of Mabel at the chalkboard, teaching a new generation of kids that "Anyone can learn to understand — and fix — the world around them."
