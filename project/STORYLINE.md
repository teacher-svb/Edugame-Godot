## Act I: Welcome to Beaverford (setup)

Mabel lives in Beaverford, a small village in a faraway land. In the forest near Beaverford lies a source of magic, the mana spring, which allows wizards to cast spells throughout the entire land. This spring is closely guarded.

The people of Beaverford use magic to solve everything, from minor annoyances to major problems. For this they rely on the support of a wizard in the village. He occasionally lets them down, causing the backlog of broken things to grow ever longer.

Mabel wants to help the people in her village, but everyone turns her away. If she wants to help, her fellow villagers say, she needs to learn to use magic. So she signs up for the wizard school, where she visits several wizards to learn the different types of magic. But what does she find? Mabel cannot do magic at all. Defeated, she returns to her village.

## Act II: Spellcasting Without a Wand (hook)

Back in the village, she happens to meet a villager she actually can help. Not with magic, but with mathematical and physical insight. The villager is astonished, and tells the whole village that she can cast spells without a wand! And so Mabel makes her way around the village, helping to fix all sorts of things.

Everything seems to be going well, until one of the wizards from the wizard school passes through the village. He declares that Mabel cannot do magic at all! The villagers are completely confused and feel misled. When the wizard leaves, Mabel goes back home. There she finds a mysterious letter.

## Act III: The Guild of Engineers (development)

The letter is an invitation from the secret guild of engineers. They have heard how well she can fix things (without magic!), and ask whether she would like to become an engineer apprentice at their secret workshop.
There she learns all kinds of new things. Armed with this new knowledge, she ventures out at night into the village, secretly fixing increasingly difficult problems. The villagers assume the village wizard is hard at work, as they haven't seen him in quite some time.

During her nightly missions she begins to notice that the village's magical lighting is growing dimmer and occasionally flickering. More and more things are breaking down, and all magical objects are failing with increasing frequency. What on earth is happening to the magic?

## Act IV: The Secret of the Wizards (turn)

It has now gone completely dark. But Mabel can build a lamp that needs no magic. With that lamp she sets out to find the mana spring. Because it has grown so dark, the entrance is no longer guarded.

In the middle of the forest Mabel finds a clearing where nothing grows anymore. At its centre she finds a mine. Here mana is being extracted from the ground. When she enters the mine, she is confronted with the truth: the wizard is the mysterious leader of the engineers guild, who has been using machines to draw mana from the earth.

The wizard explains that the mana in this mine is nearly exhausted, and that he needs help finding a new source. He genuinely believes the world is better with magic — that it is the natural order of things, and that engineering is only a means to preserve it. Mabel realises, however, that all the technology and knowledge in the mine would be better used to address the needs of the village directly, rather than keeping everyone so dependent on magic. Finding a new source would damage the forest even further! She sees the full picture: the wizard has kept the village dependent on him twice over — through magic, and through a guild that answers only to him. In neither world was anyone ever meant to truly understand.

The wizard cannot accept this. Unable to watch the village abandon magic, he leaves alone to search for a new mana source somewhere else in the world. The next morning, his house is empty.

## Act V: Everyone Can Do Magic (resolution)

She returns to the village and builds new machines there to replace the magical solutions. The people of the village learn how to fix things themselves. The wizard school is transformed into an engineering school. And Mabel? She goes there to teach.
